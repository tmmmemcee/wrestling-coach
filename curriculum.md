# 3-Week Youth Wrestling Curriculum (Ages 6-12)

## Curriculum Philosophy
- **Progressive Skill Building**: Each lesson builds on the previous
- **3-5 Videos Per Lesson**: Short, focused practice sessions
- **Beginner-Friendly**: All videos rated "beginner" difficulty
- **Folkstyle Focus**: All techniques are folkstyle wrestling specific

---

## WEEK 1: FOUNDATIONS

### Lesson 1: Stance, Motion & Balance
**Goal**: Establish proper wrestling stance and movement fundamentals

**Videos**:
1. **Wrestling Stance For Beginners** (Kyler Larkin) - ID: 26
   - Notes: Core foundation - practice in front of mirror
2. **The Essentials of a Wrestling Stance** (Wrestling University) - ID: 126
   - Notes: Focus on feet position and balance
3. **Stance and Motion Drills For Wrestling** (The School of Wrestling) - ID: 128
   - Notes: Practice drills: 3 sets of 30 seconds each
4. **Practice Proper Wrestling Stance and Motion!** (Championship Productions) - ID: 127
   - Notes: Common mistakes to avoid

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Knees bent, back straight
- Hands up and ready
- Eyes on opponent's chest

---

### Lesson 2: Level Change & Penetration Step
**Goal**: Learn the fundamental shooting motion

**Videos**:
1. **LEVEL CHANGE in WRESTLING - What NOT to do...** (Ironclad Wrestling) - ID: 130
   - Notes: Understanding common mistakes first
2. **Wrestling Basics - Penetration Step by Adam Wheeler** (Fanatic Wrestling) - ID: 29
   - Notes: The #1 most important skill for takedowns
3. **Split Step Level Change - Cary Kolat** - ID: 131
   - Notes: Advanced detail but teaches proper timing
4. **Penetration Step Power Leg - Cary Kolat** - ID: 134
   - Notes: Focus on the power leg

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Level change = bend knees, not waist
- Penetration step should be explosive
- Keep head up while shooting
- Practice 10 reps each side

---

### Lesson 3: Hand Fighting & Ties
**Goal**: Learn to control ties and create scoring opportunities

**Videos**:
1. **The Hand Fighting Blueprint Every Wrestler Needs** (Martial Breakdowns) - ID: 33
   - Notes: Core concepts of hand fighting
2. **Hand Fighting Head Control - Cary Kolat** - ID: 136
   - Notes: Head control = control the match
3. **Jordan Burroughs Hand Fighting Mastery** (RUDIS) - ID: 142
   - Notes: Learn from the best - watch his timing

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Control the inside position
- Keep elbows tight
- Use head as a weapon
- Create angles for shots

---

## WEEK 2: TAKEDOWNS & DEFENSE

### Lesson 4: Single Leg Takedown
**Goal**: Master the most fundamental takedown in wrestling

**Videos**:
1. **How to SHOOT the PERFECT Single Leg for Beginners!** (TeachMeGrappling) - ID: 2
   - Notes: Best beginner breakdown available
2. **5 Minutes Of Pure Wrestling SINGLE LEG Technique** (United World Wrestling) - ID: 66
   - Notes: Watch different finishes
3. **Single Leg Defense Basics - The power of the Whizzer** (WrestlingTournaments) - ID: 116
   - Notes: Learn defense while learning offense

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Penetration step to outside
- Grab behind the knee
- Put head on inside
- Drive through the leg

---

### Lesson 5: Double Leg Takedown
**Goal**: Learn wrestling's most explosive takedown

**Videos**:
1. **Double Leg Take Down Basics** (Iron Faith Wrestling) - ID: 4
   - Notes: Simple, clear instruction
2. **Double Leg, Youth Wrestling Technique** (Adam Wheeler) - ID: 68
   - Notes: Specifically for youth wrestlers
3. **How to Shoot in Wrestling - Penetration Step & Level Change** (Prime Wrestling System) - ID: 135
   - Notes: Combines all Week 1-2 skills

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Change level BEFORE shooting
- Head to the side, not chest
- Grab behind both knees
- Drive and lift

---

### Lesson 6: Sprawling & Takedown Defense
**Goal**: Learn to stop shots and create counter opportunities

**Videos**:
1. **Wrestling Moves - Sprawl by Ben Askren** (BJJ Fanatics) - ID: 21
   - Notes: Best sprawl instruction available
2. **Wrestling Sprawl Secrets by John Smith** (Fanatic Wrestling) - ID: 114
   - Notes: John Smith's legendary defense
3. **Best Sprawl Position To Stop Leg Attack - Cary Kolat** - ID: 20
   - Notes: Proper hip positioning

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Hips DOWN when opponent shoots
- Hands on opponent's shoulders
- Spin behind for 2 points
- Never get flattened out

## WEEK 3: GROUND WRESTLING

### Lesson 7: Stand-Up Escape
**Goal**: Master the most important escape from bottom position

**Videos**:
1. **Stand Up Escape: Basic Bottom Wrestling Moves** (Fundamental Wrestling) - ID: 95
   - Notes: The foundation of all escapes
2. **How To Get Away From EVERYONE (bottom wrestling)** (David Carr) - ID: 18
   - Notes: NCAA champion's secrets
3. **Why 99% of Beginners Get Stuck on Bottom** (Iron Faith Wrestling) - ID: 94
   - Notes: Common mistakes to avoid

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Create space first
- Stand up in one motion
- Hand control is key
- Don't get pulled back down

---

### Lesson 8: Sit-Out & Switch
**Goal**: Learn alternative escapes when stand-up isn't available

**Videos**:
1. **Bottom Wrestling- sit out, turn in, out back door** (Team Central Wrestling) - ID: 98
   - Notes: Multiple escape options
2. **Sit Out Hip Heist Recovery From Breakdown - Cary Kolat** - ID: 105
   - Notes: Proper hip movement
3. **Wrestling Reversals: The Switch** (DICK'S) - ID: 107
   - Notes: Classic reversal technique

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Sit-out when opponent rides high
- Turn in to face opponent
- Switch when opponent reaches
- Always be moving on bottom

---

### Lesson 9: Half Nelson Pin
**Goal**: Learn wrestling's most basic and effective pinning combination

**Videos**:
1. **Youth wrestling half Nelson pin** (MaverickWrestling) - ID: 13
   - Notes: Specifically for youth wrestlers
2. **Half Nelson Tutorial | Youth Wrestling Pin from Top** (Exposure Youth Wrestling) - ID: 14
   - Notes: Step-by-step breakdown
3. **My Half Nelson Tutorial | Wrestling Fundamentals** (Coach Eby) - ID: 143
   - Notes: Common variations

**Key Teaching Points**:
- Control the wrist first
- Slide arm under arm
- Apply pressure with forearm
- Walk toward opponent's head

## Practice Structure Template

Each 60-minute practice:

**Warm-up (10 min)**:
- Stance and motion drills
- Level change exercises
- Hand fighting pummeling

**Technique Instruction (15 min)**:
- Watch videos as a group
- Coach demonstrates key points
- Q&A

**Drill Work (25 min)**:
- Partner drills (3-4 rotations)
- 3-5 reps each position
- Coach feedback

**Live Situations (10 min)**:
- Situation-specific wrestling
- Focus on day's technique

## Notes for Coaches

1. **Repetition is key**: Each technique should be drilled 50+ times before moving on
2. **Partner carefully**: Match skill levels for live drilling
3. **Safety first**: Always emphasize proper technique over aggression
4. **Make it fun**: Use games and competitions to keep kids engaged
5. **Progress tracking**: Note which kids need extra help with specific techniques

This curriculum covers the essential skills every youth wrestler needs to become competitive.
